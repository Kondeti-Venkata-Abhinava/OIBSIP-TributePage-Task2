About Swami Vivekananda

Swami Vivekananda was a Hindu monk, philosopher, and spiritual leader who is credited with introducing Indian philosophies like Vedanta and Yoga to the Western world. He was born on January 12, 1863, in Kolkata, India, and his birth name was Narendranath Datta. Vivekananda was a disciple of Sri Ramakrishna, and he founded the Ramakrishna Mission and Ramakrishna Math in 1897. He passed away on July 4, 1902, at the age of 39.

Teachings of Swami Vivekananda

Swami Vivekananda's teachings emphasized the importance of self-realization, the unity of all religions, and the service of humanity. He believed that everyone has the potential for spiritual growth and that the purpose of life is to achieve self-realization. Vivekananda encouraged his followers to be fearless and to develop a strong character. He also emphasized the importance of education and believed that education was the key to overcoming poverty and ignorance.

"Arise, awake and stop not till the goal is reached" - Swami Vivekananda
